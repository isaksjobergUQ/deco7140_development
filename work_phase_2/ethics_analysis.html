Ethics Assessment

This page looks at the ethics of the newsletter subscription feature. We focus on possible harms, then suggest fixes based on the ACS Code of Ethics.

Potential Harms

  • Collecting too much personal info (income, sexuality) that isn’t needed.
  • People feel forced to give details just to sign up.
  • Risk of data leaks if the plug-in isn’t secure.
  • Using data for ads or other purposes without clear consent.
  • Stress for users if the form is long or confusing.

Values, Principles & Solutions

  • Public Interest & Honesty: Only ask for what’s needed (email, maybe name). Be clear about why.
  • Quality of Life: Keep the form short, simple, and easy to understand.
  • Competence: Use secure storage and give people an easy way to unsubscribe or delete data.
  • Professionalism: No hidden opt-outs or dark patterns. Always respect user choice.

Why this matters

For Priya, less data means faster sign-up on mobile. For Hana, a simple form reduces stress. For Ava, clear purpose and consent build trust.
